01 · Brand Identity

Who we are.
Why it matters.

STAR Dynamics is a behavioural science consultancy with a singular focus: making the science of human behaviour genuinely useful to the people who need it most. We are not a modest iteration on what came before. We are the successor to it.

The STAR Framework synthesises seven established psychological theories into four primary mindsets and twelve distinct archetypes. It sits at the intersection of academic rigour and everyday practicality, and was designed from the ground up for the AI era, not retrofitted to it.

Where DISC, Insights Discovery, and MBTI were built for a world that no longer quite exists, STAR was built for the world as it is today and the one that is fast arriving. The future of understanding human behaviour is dynamic, context-sensitive, and AI-ready. Our entire framework is built around those principles.

The Human Operating System is not merely a tagline. It is a precise description of what STAR does: it gives organisations and individuals a consistent, evidence-based architecture for understanding how people think, decide, and act.

"The tools that dominated the last three decades were genuinely valuable in their time. Their time is passing. STAR was built for what comes next."— Positioning Statement
Our Positioning

STAR Dynamics is the next generation of behavioural science in practice. Built on solid academic foundations, designed for the AI age, and created for leaders, communicators, and organisations who refuse to be left behind by the future.

05 · Voice & Tone

Consistent voice.
Adaptive tone.

Brand voice is consistent. Tone adapts. The underlying character of how STAR Dynamics communicates remains the same whether we are writing a Substack essay, presenting at a conference, or submitting a client proposal. Tone shifts naturally depending on context. The personality underneath never wavers.
The Five Pillars of the STAR Voice
1

Visionary but Grounded

We speak about the future with confidence and genuine excitement, not as something vague and distant but as something that is already arriving. Our optimism is earned because it is anchored in evidence, in psychology, in data. We look ahead clearly because we understand what is already here. Confidence without evidence is bluster. Evidence without confidence is inertia. STAR communicates neither.

2

Authoritative without Arrogance

STAR Dynamics knows its subject deeply, and it shows. We write and speak with confidence because our foundations are solid. But authority in our voice never tips into condescension. We are the expert colleague who has spent years studying exactly this, not the professor who marks papers with a red pen. We share what we know generously, and we hold our position firmly, but we never talk down.

3

Warm and Intellectually Generous

Behavioural science, done well, is an act of empathy. STAR Dynamics communicates that warmth. We are genuinely curious about people, and that curiosity comes through in how we write and speak. We share ideas freely, building understanding rather than gatekeeping it. The radical generosity model — open knowledge-sharing as the primary authority-building strategy — is a fundamental brand principle, not a marketing tactic.

4

Provocative in the Best Sense

We are not afraid to challenge conventional wisdom, including the conventional wisdom of an industry we are actively disrupting. We say clearly that the old frameworks are showing their age, and that the future belongs to organisations willing to think differently. We do this with conviction and evidence, never contempt. The comparison to legacy tools is fair and encouraged — but always framed as evolution, not attack. "The next generation" not "the competition."

5

Clear and Energetic

We do not hide behind jargon. We do not make simple ideas complicated to appear more intelligent. The STAR Framework is sophisticated in its construction and clear in its expression. Sentences have energy. Paragraphs build momentum. Good ideas deserve to be written in a way that makes them irresistible to read. If a paragraph can be cut without losing meaning, it should be cut.

07 · Channel Guidance

Same voice.
Different register.

Brand voice is consistent across every channel. Tone adapts to the platform, the audience, and the purpose. A Substack essay and a LinkedIn post are written by the same author — but not in the same register. Understanding the difference is the practical application of the STAR voice.
Substack
Reflective · Expansive · Generous
Long-form essays that reward sustained attention. Ideas explored at full length with confidence and intellectual generosity. The register is conversational but substantive — the best Substack pieces feel like a letter from a brilliant colleague who has spent the last week thinking about one important thing and is now ready to tell you what they found.
"The reason most personality frameworks fail in practice is not complexity — it is a fundamental misunderstanding of how humans actually make decisions."
LinkedIn
Confident · Punchy · Thought-provoking
Direct observations that invite debate. Optimistic and forward-looking with a clear point of view on every post. The opening line must earn the click to see more. The register is authoritative but accessible — the best LinkedIn posts from STAR feel like the opening of a talk you immediately want to attend. Short paragraphs. No jargon. One big idea, well expressed.
"DISC had its moment. MBTI had its era. The next decade belongs to frameworks built for the AI age."
Keynotes & Talks
High energy · Narrative-driven · Inclusive
The room should feel part of the journey. Moments of wit balanced with depth. Concrete examples over abstract claims. The best STAR keynote makes the audience feel that they have learned something genuinely useful and immediately actionable — and that they recognised themselves somewhere in it. Open with a hook that creates a question the rest of the talk answers.
"I want to tell you about four types of people. And I guarantee you will recognise every single one of them."
Client Proposals
Precise · Confident · Professionally warm
The language of a trusted expert, not a vendor. Lead with the outcome the client actually wants, then demonstrate how the STAR Framework delivers it. The science serves the story, not the other way around. Proposals should feel like they were written specifically for this client — because they should be. Generic language is a failure of attention, and clients notice.
"The STAR Framework gives your organisation a shared language for human behaviour — one that is rigorous, practical, and built for the challenges you are actually facing."
Media & Press
Bold thesis · Credible voice · Accessible depth
Give journalists and editors a hook they cannot ignore, then reward their attention with real substance. Be bold with the thesis. Name the disruption clearly. Journalists are looking for experts who can hold a perspective with confidence and make complex ideas genuinely accessible. STAR is that expert. Sound like it.
"Personality typing had its era. The STAR Framework is what replaces it — and it was designed from the ground up for a world that the old tools were never built to understand."
Email Outreach
Direct · Personal · Evidence-led
Never cold. Always specific. The best STAR outreach emails demonstrate that the sender has actually engaged with the recipient's work, identifies a specific connection to the STAR Framework, and makes a single, clear ask. One email, one ask. The CPR profile is a demonstration tool, not an attachment to send without context. Use it live, as a conversation opener, not a cold drop.
"I read your piece on leadership and psychological safety last month — and it maps almost perfectly onto something the STAR Framework predicts about Aligner-mode leaders under pressure."
08 · Business Outreach

Reaching the right people.
In the right way.

STAR Dynamics operates a radical generosity model as its primary authority-building strategy. Open knowledge-sharing, not gated content, builds the trust that converts into commercial relationships. Outreach follows from reputation, not the other way around.
Primary Audiences
B2B Clients

Senior Leaders & Organisations

HR directors, L&D professionals, CMOs, executive teams, marketing strategists. Almost certainly familiar with DISC or MBTI. Sceptical of another framework, time-pressured, and hungry for tools that work in the real world. They need evidence of rigour but also need to feel that STAR genuinely understands their challenges.

Lead with outcome. Make the science serve the story.

Publishers & Media

Journalists, Editors & Platforms

Journalists, podcast hosts, conference organisers, editors, industry commentators. Looking for experts who can make complex ideas accessible without dumbing them down, and who hold a perspective with confidence. A strong point of view and a story worth telling are the entry requirements.

Be bold with the thesis. Reward attention with substance.

The Outreach Process
1

Build Reputation First

Consistent, generous, high-quality content on Substack and LinkedIn establishes the STAR voice as an authority before any direct outreach begins. The Substack/LinkedIn Tuesday, Thursday, Saturday cadence creates the platform from which commercial relationships emerge. Outreach cold is weak. Outreach from an established platform is entirely different.

2

Earn the Meeting with Specificity

Every outreach message should demonstrate that you have actually read, watched, or engaged with the target's work. Reference something specific. Identify a genuine connection between their stated challenges and the STAR Framework. The email should feel like a natural next step in a conversation that is already happening, not an interruption to one that isn't.

3

Use the CPR as a Live Demonstration Tool

The Core Profile Report (CPR) is not a cold email attachment. It is a live demonstration instrument. Hold it back until the conversation. When used in a call — where the framework can be explained, the profile interpreted, and the science unpacked in real time — it demonstrates the power of STAR in a way that a PDF never can. Show, don't just describe.

4

Make One Clear Ask

Every outreach communication should make a single, specific ask. A 20-minute call. A review of a specific piece of work. An introduction to one named person. Vague asks produce vague responses. Specific asks produce specific responses — including specific rejections, which are still more useful than silence.

5

Follow Up Once, Clearly

One follow-up after initial outreach, after a reasonable interval (five to seven working days). The follow-up should be brief, re-state the ask, and offer a specific alternative if the original timing doesn't work. Never three or more follow-ups. Persistence becomes pressure, and pressure destroys the warm authority STAR Dynamics has built.

Outreach Contexts
Strategic Partnerships

AI & Technology Platforms

Position STAR as the missing psychological personalisation layer for AI platforms. The architecture of the STAR Framework — four mindsets, twelve archetypes, operating modes — is directly translatable into AI personalisation models. Lead with this structural argument, not with assessment products.

Corporate Clients

L&D & HR Functions

Lead with the outcome: a shared language for human behaviour that reduces friction, improves communication, and gives leaders tools they can use from day one. Demonstrate credibility through specific references to the framework's academic foundation. Avoid the word "workshop" unless the client uses it first.

Speaking

Conferences & Keynotes

The NYC Big Book Award citation (2025, Marketing & Public Relations) provides credibility without fanfare. Propose a specific talk title with a clear audience problem it solves. Conference organisers select speakers who solve specific problems for specific audiences, not those with the most impressive general credentials.

Publishing

Media & Thought Leadership

The two published books (The STAR Framework and The STAR Operating System) anchor all media outreach. Lead with a specific thesis, not a biography. Journalists commission pieces from experts with a clear point of view on a timely subject. Always connect the STAR perspective to a live cultural, organisational, or technological conversation.

Assessment Products

Individual & Team Clients

The product ladder runs from CPR (£49) through Full Spectrum Report (£200) to Complete Journey (£550) and Full Journey (£750). Never lead with product pricing. Lead with the outcome the assessment delivers — self-knowledge, team cohesion, communication precision — and let the pricing emerge from the value conversation, not precede it.

Sector Exclusions

What Not to Reference

Student accommodation and the Homes for Students / VervLife / Orla sector are excluded from all STAR Dynamics public-facing communications. The Tension Engine is excluded from all public-facing documents. Neither should appear in any outreach, proposal, or published material under the STAR Dynamics brand.

How to describe
what STAR is.

A few principles should govern any communication about the STAR Framework itself — whether in a client pitch, a Substack article, a media interview, or a casual professional conversation. Consistency in how we describe the framework is as important as consistency in how we present it visually.

"A behavioural science framework that synthesises seven established psychological theories into four primary mindsets and twelve professional archetypes — designed for the AI age."

"The STAR Framework is the Human Operating System for anyone who works with people. Built on seven validated psychological theories, it gives leaders, communicators, educators, and designers a shared, evidence-based language for understanding how people think, decide, and act."

What STAR Dynamics
never sounds like.

"When in doubt, ask the simplest test question: does this sound like the future? If the answer is no, it needs another pass."
The Test

Read any piece of STAR content back aloud. If it could have been written by McKinsey, by a generic L&D consultancy, or by an AI that was asked to "write something inspiring about people and business" — it is not STAR writing. Our voice is distinctive, purposeful, and unmistakably forward-facing. Generic is the one failure with no excuse.
